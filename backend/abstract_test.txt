Recent advances in natural language processing have highlighted the potential of hybrid extractive–abstractive summarization techniques for improving academic literature review workflows. In this study, we propose a framework that integrates graph-based ranking algorithms with transformer-based sequence models to automatically identify and condense salient information from large collections of scientific texts. Using a dataset of multidisciplinary research articles, we evaluate the system’s ability to generate coherent summaries that balance factual accuracy with readability. Experimental results demonstrate that the hybrid approach outperforms baseline extractive methods in terms of semantic coverage and user satisfaction, while maintaining computational efficiency suitable for real-time applications. These findings suggest that combining statistical and neural strategies can significantly enhance automated knowledge synthesis, offering practical benefits for researchers navigating increasingly complex scholarly domains.
