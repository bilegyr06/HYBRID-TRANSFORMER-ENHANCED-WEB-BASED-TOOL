The rapid growth of scientific literature has made manual review increasingly difficult for researchers. 
Traditional methods rely on reading abstracts and full texts, which is time-consuming and prone to bias. 
Recent advances in natural language processing offer automated solutions through extractive and abstractive summarization. 
Extractive techniques like TextRank select important sentences based on graph centrality. 
Abstractive models such as BART generate novel summaries but risk hallucinations without grounding. 
This work proposes a hybrid approach combining TextRank for faithful extraction with fine-tuned transformers for coherent multi-document abstraction. 
Experiments on arXiv abstracts show improved ROUGE scores and theme coverage compared to single-method baselines.
