Climate change poses significant challenges to global food security, yet many current models fail to capture the complexity of regional adaptation strategies.
This paper introduces a multi‑agent simulation framework that integrates economic, ecological, and social variables to predict agricultural resilience under varying climate scenarios.
Our experiments reveal that mid‑sized farming communities adopting cooperative resource‑sharing outperform both small independent farms and large industrial operations in long‑term sustainability.
Interestingly, the results also show that technological innovation alone is insufficient without parallel investment in social infrastructure.
We conclude by highlighting policy implications for governments seeking to balance innovation funding with community‑level support programs.
