The legal case documents are very long, complex and of different structure. It is very time consuming to make sense out of these documents manually. Thus, there is a need to automatically generate summary of these documents so that legal experts, professionals, and clients who need help with similar cases can find them easily. Using Natural Language Processing for summarizing the legal documents can help close this gap by making it possible to analyze them faster and more accurately. This study proposes a hybrid approach for analysis and summarization of legal documents. It integrates extractive and abstractive techniques. TextRank and LexRank are used for extractive summarization to identify and extract the sentences, while Legal Pegasus is employed for abstractive summarization to generate summaries by understanding the meaning and context of the content. The generated summaries are human readable paraphrased. In the proposed approach, TextRank and Legal Pegasus are integrated to improve the performance. To measure the performance of the proposed system, we utilize the ROUGE metric, which reports the F1-score. This is done by comparing the summaries generated and the reference summaries provided in the dataset. Experimental results show that TextRank achieves ROUGE-1, ROUGE-2, and ROUGE-L scores of $0.2159,0.1633$, and 0.1366, respectively, while LexRank provides 0.1966, 0.1071, and 0.127. Legal Pegasus provides a lower value of $0.2035,0.0468$, and 0.128 but produces more comprehensible summaries. The proposed approach which integrates TextRank and Legal Pegasus outperforms the individual TextRank and Legl Pegasus models. It achieves ROUGE-1, ROUGE-2, and ROUGE-L scores of 0.2795, 0.1383, and 0.1677, thus contributing improved structural preservation and content overlap. The proposed solution can be used by legal experts, and law firms for reducing the effort and time required for manual review.
